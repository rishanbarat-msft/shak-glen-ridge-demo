Our Story

Born in a public park.

Shake Shack didn’t start as a chain. It started as a neighborhood favor.

2001 — A Summer Hot Dog Cart

Set up by Danny Meyer in Madison Square Park to support a public art installation. Not a business plan — a neighborhood gesture.

2004 — The First Shack Opens

A permanent kiosk — designed to give back to the park that hosted us. The community made us; we didn’t make the community.

Today — 550+ Shacks Worldwide

Still operated like a neighborhood spot. Still rooted in the place we came from. Each one hand-built, hand-staffed, hand-picked.

Stand for something good — fine ingredients, kind service, real community. A Shack should make a neighborhood better than it found it. — Our founding principle

Why Glen Ridge

You’re the kind of town
we build for.

We’ve turned down more locations than we’ve opened. Glen Ridge isn’t a fit by accident — it’s a fit by design.

A walkable Main Street.

Glen Ridge is one of the most pedestrian-friendly boroughs in Essex County. Our smaller-format Shacks are designed for sidewalks, not parking lots.

A family town.

More than 70% of Glen Ridge households include children. Shake Shack is a Saturday-after-soccer kind of place.

A district worth honoring.

Your Historic District covers more than 90% of the Borough. We see that as a feature — not a hurdle. We’ve designed inside districts before.

We’ve Been Listening

Here’s what neighbors
usually ask. We have answers.

“Will it bring traffic?”

Our typical Shack draws 60% of guests on foot or bike when sited near a walkable district. An independent traffic study will be filed with the application.

“Will it look out of place?”

No. The facade, signage, lighting, and materials will be drawn to your Historic Design Guidelines and reviewed under a Certificate of Appropriateness.

“What about noise & smell?”

Closed-system kitchen ventilation with charcoal filtration. No outdoor speakers, no drive-thru. We close at 10 PM on weekends.

“What about signage?”

No internally-lit cabinets. Halo-lit, gooseneck-lamp, or painted-wood signs only — chosen with the HPC.

Have a question we haven’t answered? Bring it tonight — that’s why we’re here.

Designed for Glen Ridge

Not a prototype.
A neighbor.

Illustrative concept — subject to HPC review.

Brick over panel.

Local red brick to match the streetscape — not branded paneling. Every material chosen to defer to the historic character of Bloomfield Avenue.

Awning, not billboard.

A single canvas awning in our green; signage scaled to the block. We don’t shout — we fit in.

Lamp posts, not floodlights.

Gas-style fixtures and warm 2700K LEDs — no parking-lot glare. Evening ambiance that enhances, not overwhelms.

Planters and benches.

We add to the sidewalk; we don’t take from it. Native plantings, public seating, and a streetscape that serves everyone.

Every Shack is custom-drawn. Compare our Madison Square original, our Hudson Yards rail-shed, and our Asbury Park boardwalk Shack — three blocks, three buildings, one brand.

Honoring the Historic District

We come ready for your
process — not around it.

01

Pre-Application

Informal HPC walk-through with our architect before drawings are finalized.

02

Regular Application

Filed 20+ days before HPC. Full digital package. Neighbor notice within 200 ft.

03

Public Hearing

Architect of record present in-person. We listen, we revise, we return if asked.

04

COA Issued

No exterior work begins until the Certificate of Appropriateness is issued and posted.
